# Walkthrough

I started by identifying the core pain point: the team manually answers the same marketing performance question over and over, and the response varies depending on who does it.

I chose to scope a lightweight internal briefing tool instead of a broad platform because the brief says the team wants something faster, more consistent, and less dependent on one person. That means the first version should reduce manual reporting effort, not replace every existing marketing workflow.

I defined the primary users as internal analysts and account managers because they are the people most likely to assemble and use the answer. I treated the client as a secondary user because the output could eventually be shared externally, but the main workflow is internal.

For version 1, I kept the scope focused on client selection, date range selection, channel summaries, and simple insights. I deliberately excluded forecasting, optimization, and a full client portal because those would make the first version more complex without solving the main problem.

I also included trust factors like source names and timestamps because users need to know where the numbers came from and whether they are current. If I had more time, I would validate whether the best output is a dashboard, a generated brief, or both.
